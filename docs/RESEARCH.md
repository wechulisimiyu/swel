# Copywriting & UX Research: Academic Health-Equity Sites

Conducted 2026-06-09. Audited 6 comparable sites to establish the industry standard for
academic research lab and global-health institution websites. Delivers actionable copy
patterns, UX conventions, and trust signal benchmarks for SWEL's redesign.

## 1. Tone & Voice

| Pattern | Sites Using | Example | SWEL Implication |
|---------|-------------|---------|------------------|
| **First-person plural ("we")** | PGSSC, Duke, GSF, Op Smile, HGC | "We work to achieve health equity..." (Duke), "We diligently brought..." (Op Smile) | Dominant pattern. SWEL should use "we" as primary voice. |
| **Third-person mission statements** | All 6 in formal sections | "The Program... is a vibrant community" (PGSSC) | Mix: "we" for body, third-person for formal positioning. |
| **Plain language first** | Duke, Op Smile, HGC | "Health is a human right" vs jargon | SWEL's current copy is already plain. Keep this. |
| **Statement-of-fact data lead** | GSF, HGC, Op Smile | "5 billion people lack surgical care" (GSF), "500k+ Patients Treated" (Op Smile) | SWEL's "Over 5 billion people lack access" follows this — good. |
| **Personal narrative** | Op Smile, Duke | Patient stories ("Sebastian's Long Road"), "Voices of DGHI" | SWEL needs this — currently absent. |
| **Urgency without alarmism** | GSF, Op Smile | "No one should die for a lack of access" (GSF) | SWEL's current tone is too passive. Add urgency. |

**Recommendation:** Use "we" as primary voice, lead sections with data, and add 1-2
personal narratives (researcher profiles, beneficiary stories).

## 2. Tagline / Value Prop Patterns

| Site | Hero Tagline | Pattern |
|------|-------------|---------|
| PGSSC | *None — jumps to quote then fellowship* | Weak (Wix limitation) |
| Duke GHI | "We work to achieve health equity for vulnerable groups and individuals around the world through research, education and partnership." | Mission-as-tagline — 1 sentence, 3 pillars |
| Op Smile | "Catch the Joy of Giving New Smiles" | Emotional appeal — action-oriented |
| GSF | "No one should die for a lack of access to surgical care" | Problem-as-hook — bold, memorable |
| HGC | "Creating Hope in Conflict: A Humanitarian Grand Challenge" | Name + Purpose — descriptive |
| SWEL (current) | "Advancing and Designing" | Generic — lacks specificity |

**Industry standard pattern:** Problem hook → what we do → proof → CTA.

**Recommended SWEL hero drafts:**

1. **Problem-led:** "5 billion people lack safe surgery. We're building the evidence to change that."
2. **Mission-led:** "Advancing gender equity in global surgery — through research, advocacy, and systems change."
3. **Action-led:** "Surgical equity, proven. Research that drives policy and transforms care."

## 3. CTA Language

| Site | Primary CTA | Secondary CTA | Pattern |
|------|------------|---------------|---------|
| PGSSC | "CLICK HERE FOR MORE INFORMATION" | "DOWNLOAD HERE" | Too generic — caps feel dated |
| Duke GHI | "Learn More" | "Sign Up" (newsletter) | Standard but safe |
| Op Smile | "Donate Now" | "Learn More" | Money-first — not right for SWEL |
| GSF | "EXPLORE THE SURGICAL CARE GAP" / "DONATE NOW" | "GET IN TOUCH" | Context-first, then ask — best pattern for hybrid |
| HGC | "Learn More" | "View all Innovators" | Explore-first — matches research intent |
| SWEL (current) | "EXPLORE OUR RESEARCH" | "VIEW PUBLICATIONS" / "Become a Research Partner" | Good direction — action-oriented, specific |

**Recommendation:** SWEL's current CTAs are above industry average. Specific beats
generic. Keep the pattern but make hierarchy clearer. Shorten "Become a Research
Partner" to "Join as a Research Partner."

## 4. Trust Signals

| Signal | Who Uses It | How | SWEL Status |
|--------|------------|-----|-------------|
| **Impact metrics (numbers)** | Duke (204 projects, 49 countries), Op Smile (500k+ patients), GSF (3.1M+ people, 4.5K+ certs), HGC (91 innovators, $110M) | Hero section or dedicated stats band | Has stats: 15+ countries, 42 pubs, 8M impact — good |
| **Partner logos** | GSF (12 logos), HGC (3 govt logos) | Footer or dedicated section | **Missing** |
| **Funding sources** | Duke, PGSSC, GSF, HGC | Footer or partner section | **Missing** |
| **High-profile endorsements** | GSF (Dr. Tedros/WHO, Dr. Mlambo-Ngcuka/UN Women) | Quote blocks on homepage | **Missing** |
| **Annual reports** | PGSSC ("ANNUAL REPORT 2024"), GSF, Duke | Downloadable PDF section | **Missing** (links to 404) |
| **Institutional affiliations** | Duke (Duke Health, Duke Univ), PGSSC (Harvard Med), GSF (UNITAR, Harvard) | Logo bar in footer | **Missing** |
| **Publication count** | All sites | Number prominently displayed | Shows "42 Publications" — good |
| **Newsletter/Mailing list** | Duke, PGSSC, GSF, Op Smile | Footer or dedicated section | **Missing** |

**Critical gaps for SWEL:**

1. Partner/funder logos — every comparable site has these. Non-negotiable trust signal.
2. High-profile endorsements/quotes — GSF executes this brilliantly.
3. Mailing list signup — universal in industry, currently absent.
4. Annual report link — must be resolved (currently 404).

## 5. Page-Specific UX Patterns

### 5.1 Homepage Narrative Flow

| Site | Pattern | Sections |
|------|---------|----------|
| PGSSC | Jumping — quote → anti-racism → fellowship → annual report → social | Disjointed |
| Duke GHI | Mission → News/Highlights → Map/Where → Stats → Programs → Voices → Newsletter | Structured, campus-style |
| Op Smile | Hero → Highlights → Stats → Explain problem → Map/Where → Differentiators → Patient story → Donate | Strong narrative, progressive |
| GSF | Problem stat → Solution → Focus areas → Programs → Stories → Impact → Partners → Endorsement → CTA | Best overall structure |
| HGC | Problem → Solution → Stats → Innovators → Blog → Partners | Clean, minimal |

**Industry standard flow:** Hero → Stats/impact → What we do → Featured work → Trust signals (partners/endorsements) → Newsletter → CTA

**SWEL current flow:** Hero → About+Stats → Research Focus → Projects → Think Pieces → CTA

**SWEL gaps:** No partners section, no newsletter, no endorsements, no location/map.

### 5.2 Research/Projects Page

| Site | Layout | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| PGSSC | List-based "Where We Work" + "Labs" subpages | Minimal, text-heavy |
| Duke GHI | Grid of project pages with images, search/filter | Rich, filterable |
| Op Smile | Map-based country selection → per-country stats | Geographic organization |
| GSF | "Programmes" page with 4 focus areas | Simple, icon-led |

**Recommendation for SWEL:** Grid + filter by region/topic. The stitch extraction
(`docs/pages/projects/code.html`) has a 2-column card grid — this is standard.

### 5.3 Team Page

| Site | Pattern |
|------|---------|
| PGSSC | Separate Faculty and Team pages, university-style headshots |
| Duke GHI | "People" hub with Leadership, Faculty & Staff, Faces of DGHI |
| GSF | Single Team page with headshots + titles |

**Recommendation:** Headshots + name + title + 1-sentence bio. Expand to full grid
from the current 4 preview cards.

### 5.4 Blog/Think Pieces

| Site | Pattern |
|------|---------|
| Duke GHI | "News" — hybrid of press releases, student stories, research news |
| Op Smile | "Real Life Stories" — patient narratives, photo-heavy |
| GSF | "News" — press updates, WHA coverage, annual reports |
| HGC | "News & Blogs" — reports, seed stories, event coverage |

**Industry pattern:** Mix of research updates, personal stories, event coverage,
and policy briefs. SWEL's 3 think-pieces (with dates, authors, descriptions) fits
the pattern. Expand to 6+ items and add categories.

### 5.5 Contact / Get Involved

| Site | Approach |
|------|----------|
| PGSSC | Fellowships + mailing list + contact form |
| Duke GHI | "Apply" (admissions), "Give" (donate), "Subscribe" in utility nav |
| GSF | Separate "Donate" and "Contact" paths |
| HGC | "Innovators" (funding opportunities) as main CTA |

**Recommendation for SWEL:** Split "Get Involved" for researchers, "Contact" for
general inquiries. Add mailing list signup on every page.

## Appendix: Full Site Analysis

### Harvard PGSSC (pgssc.org)

**Stack:** Wix
**Voice:** Third-person formal, quote-driven. Uses Paul Farmer quotes as emotional anchors.
**Navigation:** Deep mega-menu with 9 top-level sections, ~50+ sublinks. Overly complex for SWEL scale.
**Trust signals:** Harvard Medical School affiliation, anti-racism statement, annual report PDF, mailing list, WHO Collaborating Centre badge.
**Weaknesses:** No clear hero tagline, dated Wix layout, CTA language is generic ("CLICK HERE").

### Duke Global Health Institute (globalhealth.duke.edu)

**Stack:** Drupal (university CMS)
**Voice:** First-person plural, mission-driven. Professional but warm.
**Navigation:** Utility bar (Apply, Give, Subscribe) + main nav (About, People, Education, Research, News, Events). Clean hierarchy.
**Trust signals:** Duke university branding, impact numbers on homepage, where-we-work interactive map, student stories, newsletter signup.
**Strengths:** Excellent information architecture. The "Faces of DGHI" personal narrative section humanizes the institute.
**Pattern to steal:** 3-column stats band on hero ("204 Research Projects / 49 Countries / 326 Students").

### Operation Smile (operationsmile.org)

**Stack:** WordPress, custom theme
**Voice:** First-person plural, story-driven. Emotional without being manipulative.
**Navigation:** 4 top-level sections (Changing Lives, Expanding Care, Get Involved, About Us). Clear, scannable.
**Trust signals:** 500k+ patients treated, 6,000 volunteers, 37 countries, partner logos (Johnson & Johnson, etc.), IRS 501(c)(3) badge, Lancet features.
**Strengths:** Powerful patient narrative integration (carousel of "Recent Highlights" with before/after stories). Excellent use of hero video. Strong stats visualization.
**Pattern to steal:** "Where We Work" interactive country selector with per-country stats. "Every three minutes" urgency hook.

### Global Surgery Foundation (globalsurgeryfoundation.org)

**Stack:** Squarespace
**Voice:** First-person plural, data-led. Authoritative and direct.
**Navigation:** 5 top-level sections. Clean, not overwhelming.
**Trust signals:** 12 partner logos, WHO DG and UN Women ED endorsements, 3.1M+ people impacted, annual reports, UNITAR affiliation.
**Strengths:** Best endorsement strategy of all sites audited. The Dr. Tedros quote block is prominent and powerful. The "Our Solution" section succinctly frames the problem.
**Pattern to steal:** Endorsement quote blocks from high-profile figures. Partner logo grid. "Our Focus" 4-icon layout.

### Humanitarian Grand Challenge (humanitariangrandchallenge.org)

**Stack:** WordPress
**Voice:** First-person plural, funder-voice. Confident but not self-promotional.
**Navigation:** 5 top-level sections. Simple, focused.
**Trust signals:** UK Aid + Global Affairs Canada logos, 91 innovators funded, $110M leveraged, 27 countries.
**Pattern to steal:** "The Problem / The Solution" framing pattern. Clean stats bar with icons.

### WHO + UN Health

**Stack:** Proprietary / Sitecore
**Voice:** Third-person institutional. Authoritative.
**Pattern to steal:** Comprehensive documentation structure. Useful as a content taxonomy reference, not UX model. Overly complex for an academic lab site.
